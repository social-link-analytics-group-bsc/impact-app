By scientific impact, we understand the intellectual contribution to a personâ€™s field of study within academia [1]. Within the various methods proposed in the literature to measure the scientific impact of research (e.g., [2], [3], [4]), we decided to employ the method called *normalized impact of the scientific production*, which is the methodology employed by the government of Spain to understand the impact of publications authored by scholars working in the country's research institutions [5].

Indicators
# Name Description
1 Number of publications (NP) Number of papers published by the organization (or researcher). Papers are considered normal articles published in journals and in conference proceedings
2 Number of citations (C) Total citations received by NP
3 Mean of citations per paper (CPP) Average number of citations per publications
4 Percentage of not-cited papers (%ncP) Proportion of papers that did not receive citations
5 Percentage of self-citation (%sc) Proportion of citations in which the list of authors of the citing paper match the list of authors of the cited paper
To understand whether a certain number of citations or a specific value of citations per publication is low or high, we compare them against some reference value. The following indicators are the international reference values used to normalize the assessment of scientific impact.

# Name Description
6 Mean of field citation score (FCS) Average of citations of all papers published in all journals of the field in which the institute has been active
7 CPP/FCS Whether or not the average citations per publications is above (or below) the international average in the field
The mean of citations by field and year can be obtained from the annual report of Elsevier.

Impact Calculation
  1. Select the range of years of evaluation. There should be at least four years between the current year and the final year of the evaluation period. The average "peak" in the number of citations happens in the third to a fourth-year after publication. A five-year period is then appropriate for impact evaluation [2];
  2. Compute the indicators for each year in the selected range;
  3. Evaluate how the institution (or researcher) performs within its field of activity.

Ilustrative Example
Scientific impact of the Institution X between years 2012 and 2015
Year NP C CPP %Pnc %sc FCS CPP/FCS*
2012 70 450 6.2 3 7 3.1 2
2013 78 370 4.7 5 6 3.6 1.3
2014 81 407 5.0 7 8 3.5 1.4
2015 84 463 5.5 4 6 3.8 1.5
* Indicates whether the performance of the institution is far below (< 0.5), below (between 0.5 and 0.8), about (between 0.8 and 1.2), above (between 1.2 and 1.5) and far above (>1.5) the international impact of the field [3]

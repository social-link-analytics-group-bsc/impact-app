  1. Social Impact
  2. Methodology
By social impact, we understand as the social improvements achieved as consequence of implementing the results of a research project. Within the various methods proposed in the literature to measure the social impact of research (e.g., ), we decided to employ the method Social Impact Open Repository (SIOR), which was proposed by the EU-funded project IMPACT-EV as a method to monitoring the impact of EU framework programmes.

Indicators
# Name Description
1 Social Target There is a connection to United Nations Sustainable Development Goals, EU2020 targets or other similar official targets
2 Improvement Percentage of improvement achieved concerning the starting situation
3 Replicability The actions based on the project findings have been successfully implemented in more than one context
4 Sustainability The impact achieved has showed to be sustainable throughout time
5 Evidence published in scientific or official document Evidence of social impact is published either on scientific journals, governmental or non-governmental reports

Only evidence of impact that has connection with social targets are considered in the analysis. We only report evaluations of impact that have connection with social targets, being them the United Nations Sustainable Development Goals (SDG) or others (e.g., EU2020).

Score System
Score Description
7 The impact meets all the indicators (i.e., 3, 4, 5) and has more than or equal to 30% of improvement
6 The impact meets all the criteria and has an improvement between 20% and 29%
5 The impact meets all the criteria and has an improvement between 10% and 19%
4 The impact meets all the criteria and has some % of improvement (or the % of improvement is not available)
3 The impact has achieved some % of improvement (or the % of improvement is not available) and meets at least 2 of the other criteria (i.e., 3, 4, 5)
2 The impact has achieved some % of improvement (or the % of improvement is not available) and meets at least 1 of the other criteria
1 There is a mention of impact but the % of improvement is not available
